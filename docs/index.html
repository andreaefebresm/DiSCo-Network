network

We are at every moment tearing through the digital fabric that lines our daily lives; surrounded by organs without bodies, AI-generated identities, and algorithmic governance that is structuring new affective horizons.

We, the Digital Studies Collective (DiSCo), are a group of PhD researchers who navigate, engage with, and insist on shaping this emerging reality. We refuse to remain passive observers: in order to grapple with humanity’s increasing seduction of the digital, we need to organise collectively and continuously learn, lament, celebrate and critique the digitisation of the everyday. At the centre of our work is the bringing together of diverse perspectives resulting in radical transdisciplinary scholarship.

To date, we have organised nine training workshops in digital methodologies and in September 2025, we launched our first research conference, “Bodies in Digital Transition,” at Goldsmiths, University of London. The 3-day conference successfully brought together researchers, artists and technologists to explore transcultural perspectives of the digital in relation to embodied and material phenomena. As well as diverse theoretical, methodological, and philosophical approaches, we welcomed creative interventions and experimental presentation formats that stretched the boundaries of academia in unexpected ways.

disco journal

DiSCo Journal has been laying the groundwork for this kind of exchange since its establishment in 2021. DiSCo Journal remains our frontline as a vital counter-narrative to the unedited noise of the digital and virtual culture, that wants to cut sharp from the dusty academic repository. This annual, born-digital space lets art, theory, and method collide, moving past worn-out paradigms and stale dualisms to inhabit new critical zones of hybridity and to build third understandings.
